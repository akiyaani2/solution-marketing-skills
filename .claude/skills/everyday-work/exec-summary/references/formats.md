# Executive Summary — Output Formats

Choose the format that matches the space you have and the audience seniority. Higher seniority = shorter format.

---

## Format 1: Half-Page Summary

**Best for:** Email body, Teams message, Slack post, quick leadership FYI.
**Length:** 6-10 lines.
**When to use:** The reader needs to absorb this in under 30 seconds without opening an attachment.

```
SUBJECT: [Topic] — [Headline Takeaway]

Bottom line: [1 sentence — the single most important thing the reader needs to know]

Key points:
- [Point 1 with metric]
- [Point 2 with metric]
- [Point 3 with metric]

Action needed: [What you need from the reader, or "FYI only"]

Timeline: [Next milestone or deadline]
```

### Half-Page Rules:
- Bottom line is exactly ONE sentence. If you need two, you haven't distilled enough.
- Key points: 3 max. Each under 15 words. Quantified where possible.
- Action needed: Be explicit. "FYI only" is a valid answer. "Let me know your thoughts" is not.
- No setup paragraph. No "As you know..." No "Per our conversation..." Jump straight to the bottom line.

---

## Format 2: One-Page Summary

**Best for:** Document attachment, slide appendix, briefing prep, decision memo.
**Length:** 1 page (roughly 250-400 words).
**When to use:** The reader needs enough context to make a decision or form an opinion, but doesn't have time for the full document.

```
# [Topic] — Executive Summary

## Bottom Line
[2-3 sentences. Lead with the conclusion, not the context. State what happened and what it means.]

## Key Findings
1. **[Finding headline]**: [Evidence/metric that supports it]
2. **[Finding headline]**: [Evidence/metric]
3. **[Finding headline]**: [Evidence/metric]

## Risks
- [Risk 1]: [Mitigation in progress or proposed]
- [Risk 2]: [Mitigation]

## Recommendation
[What you're recommending and why. Be specific — "I recommend we approve the $50K budget for X because Y." Not "We should consider options."]

## Next Steps
| Action | Owner | By When |
|--------|-------|---------|
| [action] | [name] | [date] |
| [action] | [name] | [date] |

## Appendix Available
[Link or reference to the full document for those who want details]
```

### One-Page Rules:
- Bottom Line section is 2-3 sentences MAX. This is the most important part.
- Key Findings: 3-5 items. Each has a bolded headline and supporting evidence.
- Risks: Only include risks that are active and material. Don't list hypotheticals.
- Recommendation: REQUIRED. Executives don't want a menu of options — they want your recommendation with logic.
- Next Steps: Table format with owner and date. No orphan actions.

---

## Format 3: Three-Bullet Summary

**Best for:** Meeting prep for CVP+, "give me the TLDR," verbal briefing prep.
**Length:** 3 sentences.
**When to use:** Maximum compression. The reader has 10 seconds.

```
1. [What happened / the headline]
2. [Why it matters / the impact]
3. [What we need / the ask or next step]
```

### Three-Bullet Rules:
- Bullet 1 is a fact. Bullet 2 is the meaning. Bullet 3 is the action.
- Each bullet is one sentence, under 20 words.
- This format is for verbal delivery or the absolute top of a longer document.
- If you can't compress to 3 bullets, you don't understand the topic well enough yet.

---

## Choosing the Right Format

| Situation | Format | Why |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Email to VP+ | Half-Page | They scan email on mobile. Keep it tight. |
| Pre-read for a meeting | One-Page | They need enough to ask smart questions. |
| Verbal brief in a hallway | Three-Bullet | You have 30 seconds. Make them count. |
| Attachment to a longer doc | One-Page | Provides the "read this instead" option. |
| Teams/Slack message | Half-Page | Inline reading, no attachment needed. |
| Decision memo | One-Page | Needs enough context to support a decision. |
| "What should I tell my boss?" | Three-Bullet | Give them the talking points. |
