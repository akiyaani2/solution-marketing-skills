# Email Drafter

Drafts professional emails calibrated to the recipient, situation, and urgency. Handles status updates, asks/approvals, escalations, event invites, partner outreach, and follow-ups.

## Trigger Phrases

- `/email-drafter`
- `/email-drafter --to [leader] --type status-update`
- `draft an email to [name] about [topic]`
- `write a follow-up email`
- `help me ask [leader] for [approval/budget/decision]`

## Email Templates

### Status Update (to leadership)

```
Subject: [Program] Update — [Key Headline]

Hi [Name],

Quick update on [program]:

**Status:** [On track / At risk / Needs attention]

**Highlights:**
• [Win or milestone with metric]
• [Win or milestone with metric]

**Coming up:**
• [Next milestone] — [date]
• [Next milestone] — [date]

[Optional: risk or ask, keep to 1-2 sentences]

Happy to discuss in our next sync or jump on a call if helpful.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Ask / Approval Request

```
Subject: [Action Needed] [Topic] — [Deadline if applicable]

Hi [Name],

I'm writing to request [specific approval/decision/resource].

**What:** [1-2 sentences on what you need]
**Why:** [1-2 sentences on why it matters — tie to business impact]
**Cost/Scope:** [budget, timeline, or resource ask]
**Deadline:** [when you need the decision by and why]

**My recommendation:** [What you think we should do]

I've attached [supporting doc/data] for reference. Happy to walk through this in our next 1:1 or on a quick call.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

### Escalation

```
Subject: [Escalation] [Issue] — Need Your Input by [Date]

Hi [Name],

I'm escalating [issue] because [reason — it's above my decision authority / it impacts [stakeholder] / timeline risk].

**Situation:** [2-3 sentences on what's happening]
**Options:**
1. [Option A] — [Pro/Con]
2. [Option B] — [Pro/Con]
**My recommendation:** [Option X] because [reasoning]
**Impact of delay:** [What happens if we don't decide by [date]]

Can we discuss this in [our next sync / a 15-min call this week]?

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

### Partner Outreach

```
Subject: [Partnership Opportunity / Follow-up] — [Your Org] x [Their Org]

Hi [Name],

[1 sentence of context — how you know them or why you're reaching out]

I'm reaching out because [specific opportunity/proposal]. [1-2 sentences on what you're proposing and why it benefits both sides].

**Proposed next step:** [Specific ask — a 30-min call, review of a doc, intro to someone]

Would [suggested time] work for a quick conversation?

Best,
[Your name]
[Title]
```

### Follow-Up (after meeting or event)

```
Subject: Follow-up: [Meeting/Event Name] — [Date]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for [meeting/attending/presenting] [specific reference].

**Key takeaways:**
• [Takeaway 1]
• [Takeaway 2]

**Action items:**
• [You] will [action] by [date]
• [They] will [action] by [date]

**Next step:** [What happens next and when]

Let me know if I missed anything. Looking forward to [next touchpoint].

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Tone Calibration

| Recipient | Tone | Length | Key Differences |
|-----------|------|--------|----------------|
| CVP+ | Formal, concise, strategic | 3-5 sentences | Lead with impact. No fluff. |
| Sr. Director | Professional, direct | 5-8 sentences | Include recommendation. Respect their time. |
| Peers (cross-team leads) | Collaborative, casual-professional | Flexible | Be direct but friendly. Skip formality. |
| Team members | Warm, clear, actionable | Flexible | Be specific about what you need from them. |
| Partners | Professional, mutual-benefit framing | 5-10 sentences | Always frame as partnership, not request. |
| External (speakers, vendors) | Formal, appreciative | 5-8 sentences | Clear ask, easy to say yes to. |

## Writing Rules

1. **Subject line is a headline.** "[Action Needed] Budget Approval for Partner Hack — Due Friday" not "Re: Question"
2. **First sentence is the point.** "I'm writing to request X" or "Quick update on X." Never "I hope this email finds you well."
3. **Bold the key info.** Leadership scans, they don't read. Bold the status, the ask, the deadline.
4. **One email, one topic.** Don't combine a budget ask with an event update.
5. **End with a clear next step.** What do you want the reader to DO?
6. **Keep it short.** If it's more than a screen, it should be a document with an email summary.

## Gotchas

- Never send an email without a clear purpose. "FYI" emails should be rare and clearly labeled as informational.
- Escalation emails should ALWAYS include your recommendation. Don't just present the problem.
- For approval emails, make it easy to say yes: include all the info they need to decide in the email itself.
- Follow-up emails should be sent within 24 hours while context is fresh.
- Read the email from the recipient's perspective before sending. What would YOU want to see first?
- If you're unsure about tone, err on the side of more professional. You can always be more casual in the reply.
