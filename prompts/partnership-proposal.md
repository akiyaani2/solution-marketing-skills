# Partnership Proposal Outline

**Category:** Strategy & Planning
**Best for:** Business development, program managers, marketing leads pursuing joint initiatives
**Works in:** Copilot Chat, Claude.ai, ChatGPT, any AI assistant

---

## The Prompt

> Create a structured partnership proposal outline for the following initiative. Include:
>
> 1. **Executive Summary** — 3-4 sentences covering the what, why, and expected outcome
> 2. **Mutual Benefits** — What each party gains (be specific, not generic). Use a two-column format.
> 3. **Scope and Activities** — What exactly we will do together. List 4-6 concrete activities.
> 4. **Roles and Responsibilities** — Who owns what. Use a RACI-style breakdown for key activities.
> 5. **Timeline** — Phased plan with milestones (3-4 phases)
> 6. **Success Metrics** — 4-6 measurable KPIs both parties agree to track
> 7. **Investment and Resources** — What each party contributes (budget, staff, technology, content)
> 8. **Risks and Mitigations** — Top 3 risks with planned responses
> 9. **Next Steps** — 3-4 specific actions to move forward, with owners and dates
>
> Context:
> - Our organization: [your org and what you do]
> - Proposed partner: [partner org and what they do]
> - Initiative: [what you want to do together]
> - Goal: [what success looks like]
> - Timeline: [rough timing]

## Example

**Input:**
- Our organization: Cloud platform marketing team focused on developer skilling
- Proposed partner: Major GPU hardware company with a developer ecosystem
- Initiative: Co-branded hackathon series (4 virtual events over 6 months)
- Goal: 5,000 total developer registrations, 300+ cloud platform activations
- Timeline: Launch first event in Q3, complete series by Q4

**Output (abbreviated):**

### Executive Summary
A co-branded virtual hackathon series combining our cloud platform with [partner]'s GPU infrastructure, targeting developers building AI applications. Four events over six months will generate 5,000+ registrations and drive measurable cloud adoption, while giving [partner] direct access to an engaged developer audience building on their hardware.

### Mutual Benefits

| Our Organization | Partner |
|------------------|---------|
| GPU credits reduce our per-event cost by ~40% | Access to our 50K+ developer community |
| Partner brand adds credibility with AI practitioners | Lead generation for their developer program |
| Co-marketing doubles promotional reach | Real-world showcase of their hardware capabilities |
| Content library from hackathon projects | Customer success stories from winning teams |

### Scope and Activities
1. Four virtual hackathons (one per 6 weeks), each 3 days
2. Joint landing pages and registration funnels
3. Co-branded content: promotional emails, social media, blog posts
4. Partner-led technical workshops (GPU optimization, model training)
5. Joint judging panel with engineers from both organizations
6. Post-event case studies from top 3 projects per event

### Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Key Milestones |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **1. Planning** | Weeks 1-4 | Sign agreement, finalize event calendar, build landing page |
| **2. Launch** | Weeks 5-8 | First event live, measure baseline metrics |
| **3. Scale** | Weeks 9-20 | Events 2-4, optimize based on data, expand content |
| **4. Evaluate** | Weeks 21-24 | Final reporting, renewal decision, case study publication |

*(continues through Success Metrics, Investment, Risks, Next Steps)*

## Variations

- **For internal proposal:** Add: "Frame as an internal business case. Include budget justification, alignment to organizational OKRs, and the approval chain needed. Remove partner-facing language."
- **For external proposal:** Add: "Write in a tone suitable to send directly to the partner. Be professional but not stiff. Emphasize mutual value, not just what we need from them."
- **Shorter version:** "Give me a one-page partnership pitch: 2-sentence summary, 3 mutual benefits, 3 proposed activities, and 3 next steps for this partnership idea: [describe idea]"

## Tips for Better Results

- Be specific about what each side brings — vague proposals get vague responses from partners
- Include real numbers (audience size, budget range, historical performance) for a dramatically better output
- If this is competitive (multiple partners could fill this role), mention that context — the AI will sharpen the positioning
