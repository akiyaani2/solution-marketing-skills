# Social Posts from Content

**Category:** Content Creation
**Best for:** Content marketers, community managers, program managers promoting their work
**Works in:** Copilot Chat, Claude.ai, ChatGPT, any AI assistant

---

## The Prompt

> Create social media posts from this content. Generate:
>
> **LinkedIn Post (1 post)**
> - 150-200 words
> - Hook in the first line (must earn the "see more" click)
> - Include a clear takeaway or insight, not just an announcement
> - End with a call to action (register, read, comment)
> - Suggest 3-5 relevant hashtags
>
> **X/Twitter Thread (3-5 posts)**
> - Each post under 280 characters
> - First post is the hook — must stand alone
> - Build a logical sequence (problem → insight → proof → CTA)
> - Last post includes the link and CTA
>
> Tone: [professional / enthusiastic / informational]
> Link to include: [URL or "no link"]
>
> Source content:
>
> [Paste blog post, announcement, or event description here]

## Example

**Input:**
Tone: professional. Link: https://example.com/hackathon

Source: "We're hosting a 3-day virtual hackathon focused on building AI agents using cloud-native tools. Participants get free GPU credits, mentorship from industry engineers, and the chance to present to a panel of technical leaders. Last year's event had 2,000 participants and produced 14 projects that went into production. Registration is open now through April 10."

## Variations

- **For enthusiastic tone:** Add: "Use high-energy language. It is fine to use exclamation points sparingly. Emphasize opportunity and excitement."
- **For informational tone:** Add: "Stick to facts and value. No hype. Readers should feel informed, not sold to."
- **Shorter version:** "Write one LinkedIn post (under 150 words) and one tweet (under 280 characters) promoting this content. Lead with the most compelling stat or insight. Content: [paste content]"

## Tips for Better Results

- Always include at least one specific number or result — social posts with data dramatically outperform vague announcements
- The LinkedIn hook (first line) matters more than anything else; if it does not make someone want to click "see more," the post fails
- Test different hooks by asking the AI for "3 alternative opening lines for this LinkedIn post" and picking the strongest
