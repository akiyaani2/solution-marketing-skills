# Blog Post from Bullet Points

**Category:** Content Creation
**Best for:** Content marketers, technical writers, program managers
**Works in:** Copilot Chat, Claude.ai, ChatGPT, any AI assistant

---

## The Prompt

> I have rough bullet points for a blog post. Turn them into a structured, professional blog post draft with:
> - An engaging intro paragraph that hooks the reader with the key problem or opportunity
> - 3-5 clearly titled sections (use descriptive headers, not generic ones)
> - Supporting detail under each section — expand my bullets into full paragraphs
> - A conclusion with a clear call to action
> - Tone: [professional / conversational / technical] for an audience of [target reader]
> - Target length: [800 / 1200 / 1500] words
>
> Here are my bullet points:
>
> [Paste your bullet points here]

## Example

**Input:**
Tone: professional for an audience of IT decision-makers. Target length: 1200 words.

Bullet points:
- Companies spending 40% more on cloud but not seeing proportional value
- Most orgs only use 30% of cloud features they pay for
- Three patterns: lift-and-shift without optimization, over-provisioning, ignoring reserved capacity
- Solution is a cloud maturity assessment before migration
- Assessment covers architecture, cost management, security posture, operations
- Customers who assess first see 35% lower TCO in year one
- Call to action: download the assessment framework

**Output (abbreviated):**

**Title:** Why Your Cloud Bill Keeps Climbing — and the One Step Most Organizations Skip

Cloud spending across enterprises grew 29% last year, yet fewer than half of organizations report that their cloud investments are delivering expected business outcomes. The gap between spending and value is not a technology problem — it is a strategy problem...

**The Hidden Cost of Skipping the Foundation**
Most migration projects begin with urgency rather than intention. Teams lift existing workloads into the cloud without rearchitecting them for cloud-native efficiency...

**Three Patterns That Inflate Your Cloud Bill**
After analyzing hundreds of migration projects, three costly patterns emerge repeatedly...

*(continues through all sections to conclusion and CTA)*

## Variations

- **For technical audience:** Add this to the prompt: "Include specific technical details, architecture references, and code-level examples where relevant. Assume the reader builds and deploys software daily."
- **For thought-leadership audience:** Add: "Frame this as a point of view, not a how-to. Lead with an industry trend or contrarian insight. Use data to support an argument, not just inform."
- **For announcement post:** Add: "Structure as a news announcement. Lead with the what and why it matters. Include quotes from [speaker name/role]. End with availability details and next steps."

## Tips for Better Results

- The messier your bullets, the more you should specify tone and audience — the AI needs guardrails when input is rough
- Include any data points or statistics in your bullets even if unformatted — they make the output dramatically better
- If you have a specific blog you want to emulate, paste its URL or a paragraph from it and say "match this style"
