# Project Kickoff Agenda

**Category:** Strategy & Planning
**Best for:** Program managers, project leads, anyone starting a new initiative
**Works in:** Copilot Chat, Claude.ai, ChatGPT, any AI assistant

---

## The Prompt

> Create a structured kickoff meeting agenda for this project. Include:
>
> - **Meeting details:** Title, duration ([30 / 60] minutes), attendees (by role)
> - **Agenda items** with time allocations that add up correctly
> - For each agenda item: topic, owner/presenter, time, and key questions to answer
> - **Pre-reads:** 2-3 documents or links attendees should review beforehand
> - **Meeting outputs:** What we should walk out of this meeting having decided or aligned on
> - **Parking lot rules:** How we handle topics that come up but are out of scope
>
> The agenda should ensure we leave with: clear roles, agreed timeline, identified risks, and defined next steps.
>
> Project brief:
> - **Project name:** [name]
> - **Goal:** [what success looks like]
> - **Timeline:** [start and end dates]
> - **Key stakeholders:** [roles involved, not names]
> - **Known constraints:** [budget, dependencies, deadlines]

## Example

**Input:**
- Project name: Developer Hackathon — Spring Edition
- Goal: 1,500 registrations, 40% completion rate, 100+ cloud activations
- Timeline: Kickoff now, event in 8 weeks
- Key stakeholders: Program lead, marketing lead, partner contact, content lead, engineering support
- Known constraints: $30K budget, partner needs final plan by week 3, landing page requires 4-week lead time

**Output:**

## Developer Hackathon — Spring Edition: Kickoff Meeting

**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Attendees:** Program lead, marketing lead, partner contact, content lead, engineering support

---

### Pre-Reads (review before the meeting)
1. Draft event brief (scope, goals, budget) — [link]
2. Last hackathon's retrospective and metrics — [link]
3. Partner co-marketing guidelines — [link]

---

### Agenda

| Time | Topic | Presenter | Purpose |
|------|-------|-----------|---------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Welcome and meeting goals | Program lead | Set context: what we need to decide today |
| 0:05-0:15 | Project overview and success criteria | Program lead | Align on goals: 1,500 registrations, 40% completion, 100+ activations |
| 0:15-0:25 | Roles and ownership | Program lead | Confirm who owns what — marketing, content, partner liaison, tech setup |
| 0:25-0:35 | Timeline walkthrough and critical path | Program lead | Review 8-week plan. Flag the two hard deadlines: partner plan (week 3), landing page (week 4) |
| 0:35-0:45 | Budget and resource allocation | Program lead | $30K budget breakdown. Where is there flexibility? What is fixed? |
| 0:45-0:50 | Risk identification | All | Each person names their top risk. Capture on a shared list. |
| 0:50-0:55 | Open questions and parking lot | All | Capture anything out of scope for today |
| 0:55-1:00 | Next steps and action items | Program lead | Assign week-1 deliverables with owners and due dates |

---

### Meeting Outputs (what we must leave with)
- Confirmed roles and single-threaded owners for each workstream
- Validated 8-week timeline with critical path dates agreed
- Budget allocation by category (marketing, content, platform, partner)
- Top 5 risks documented with owners
- Week-1 action items assigned

### Parking Lot Rules
- Any topic that needs more than 2 minutes of discussion gets added to the parking lot
- Parking lot owner (program lead) schedules follow-ups within 48 hours
- Nothing in the parking lot blocks today's decisions

## Variations

- **For 30-minute meeting:** Add: "Compress to 30 minutes. Cut the budget discussion (handle async). Combine risk identification into the timeline walkthrough. Keep the meeting to: align on goals (5 min), confirm roles (5 min), review timeline and risks (10 min), next steps (5 min), buffer (5 min)."
- **For executive sponsor kickoff:** Add: "This is a kickoff with an executive sponsor. Focus on strategic alignment and decision rights, not operational details. Include a slide for the sponsor to confirm their expectations and escalation preferences."
- **Shorter version:** "Create a 30-minute kickoff agenda for [project]. Include: goals alignment, role confirmation, timeline review, risk flags, and next steps. Each item gets 5-6 minutes."

## Tips for Better Results

- Include known constraints in your brief — they shape the agenda more than the goals do
- Always specify the meeting duration; without it, the AI defaults to overly ambitious agendas
- The "meeting outputs" section is the most important part — define what success looks like before the meeting starts
