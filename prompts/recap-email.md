# Meeting Recap Email

**Category:** Meeting & Communication
**Best for:** Anyone who runs or attends meetings and needs to send follow-ups
**Works in:** Copilot Chat, Claude.ai, ChatGPT, any AI assistant

---

## The Prompt

> Write a professional meeting recap email based on these notes. Include:
>
> - **Subject line** that summarizes the meeting outcome (not just "Meeting Recap")
> - **Opening** — One sentence stating the purpose and date of the meeting
> - **Key Decisions** — Bulleted list of what was agreed upon
> - **Action Items** — Table with Action, Owner, and Due Date
> - **Open Items** — Anything unresolved that needs follow-up
> - **Next Meeting** — Date/time if known, or "TBD"
>
> Tone: [professional / friendly / formal]. Recipients: [internal team / external partner / leadership].
> Keep it under [200 / 300 / 500] words.
>
> Meeting notes:
>
> [Paste your meeting notes here]

## Example

**Input:**
Tone: professional. Recipients: internal team + one external partner contact. Keep under 300 words.

Notes from partner planning call, March 15:
- Reviewed joint hackathon plan for April. Agreed on virtual format, 3-day event.
- Partner will provide GPU credits (up to $50K value). We handle registration and marketing.
- Content tracks: beginner, intermediate, advanced. Partner owns advanced track content.
- Landing page needs to be live by March 28. Our web team owns this.
- Partner sending logos and brand guidelines by March 20.
- Still need to decide on keynote speaker — both sides will propose names by March 22.
- Legal reviewing co-marketing agreement, expected done by end of month.
- Next sync: March 22 at 2pm PT.

**Output:**

**Subject:** Partner Hackathon — Format Agreed, Action Items for March 28 Launch

Hi team,

Thanks for a productive planning session on March 15. Here is a summary of where we landed and what needs to happen next.

**Key Decisions**
- Virtual format confirmed, running 3 days in April
- Three content tracks: beginner, intermediate, advanced
- Partner owns advanced track content; our team owns beginner and intermediate
- Partner providing GPU credits (up to $50K value); we own registration and marketing

**Action Items**

| Action | Owner | Due |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Send logos and brand guidelines | [partner contact] | Mar 20 |
| Propose keynote speaker candidates | Both teams | Mar 22 |
| Build and publish landing page | Web team | Mar 28 |
| Complete co-marketing agreement review | Legal | Mar 31 |

**Open Items**
- Keynote speaker not yet decided — both sides proposing names by March 22
- Co-marketing agreement still in legal review

**Next Meeting:** March 22, 2:00 PM PT

Let me know if I missed anything or if any dates need adjustment.

Best,
[your name]

## Variations

- **For external recipients:** Add: "Use formal tone. Do not reference internal team names or tools. Frame action items as collaborative commitments, not assignments."
- **For leadership audience:** Add: "Lead with decisions and outcomes, not process. Keep to 150 words max. Skip the open items unless they are risks."
- **Shorter version:** "Write a 5-sentence recap email from these meeting notes. Include: what was decided, who does what next, and when we meet again. Notes: [paste notes]"

## Tips for Better Results

- Include attendee names in your notes so the AI can assign owners accurately
- Specify the recipient type — an email to your team reads very differently from one to a partner
- If the meeting had no clear decisions, say so; the AI will flag that as a gap rather than fabricate decisions
